Gradient Background Webpage

Linear Gradient Background

This section uses a linear gradient background that transitions from blue to purple to pink. Linear gradients are defined by an angle or direction and a sequence of color stops.

Linear gradients are perfect for creating modern, vibrant backgrounds that add depth and visual interest to web pages.

Radial Gradient Background

This section uses a radial gradient background that starts with yellow at the center and transitions to orange and then red at the edges. Radial gradients create a circular or elliptical gradient pattern.

Radial gradients are excellent for creating sunbursts, spotlight effects, or simply adding a different dimension to your background compared to linear gradients.

Fixed Gradient Background (Parallax Effect)

This section demonstrates a fixed gradient background with the background-attachment: fixed property. This creates a parallax-like effect where the background remains stationary while the content scrolls.

The parallax effect adds a sense of depth and can make your website more engaging and visually appealing. It's particularly effective for storytelling or showcasing important content.
